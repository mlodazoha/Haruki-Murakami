Here you can find a list of chosen books written by Haruki Murakami

"Hear the Wind Sing"

It is the first book in the "Trilogy of the Rat" series, followed by "Pinball, 1973" and "A Wild Sheep Chase." "Hear the Wind Sing" is a coming-of-age story set in Japan during the late 1960s. The novel follows an unnamed narrator, a college student, as he reflects on his experiences, friendships, and romantic relationships. It explores themes of youth, nostalgia, loneliness, and the search for meaning.

"Norwegian Wood"

Haruki Murakami's most famous and critically acclaimed novels. It was published in 1987 and became a significant success both in Japan and internationally. The story is set in the late 1960s in Tokyo and revolves around the protagonist Toru Watanabe, a college student. Toru navigates his way through relationships, particularly with two women: Naoko, a fragile and emotionally troubled young woman, and Midori, a vivacious and outgoing classmate.

"Dance, dance, dance"

The novel incorporates elements of mystery, surrealism, and magical realism, which are characteristic of Murakami's storytelling style. It delves into the protagonist's introspective journey as he encounters peculiar characters, confronts his past, and seeks to unravel the enigmas of the parallel world.

"South of the Border,West of the Sun"

Hajime, an only child, develops a close friendship in childhood with a girl named Shimamoto. They share a deep connection and understanding, but circumstances separate them as they grow older. In their adult lives, Hajime is married and has children, while Shimamoto mysteriously reappears, resurfacing memories and emotions from their past.

"The Wind-Up Bird Chronicles"

It is considered one of Murakami's most significant and complex works, blending elements of magical realism, surrealism, and psychological mystery.The story follows the protagonist, Toru Okada, as he embarks on a surreal journey after his wife, Kumiko, goes missing. As Toru searches for his wife, he encounters a range of eccentric and enigmatic characters who lead him into a labyrinth of strange occurrences and hidden truths. Along the way, he discovers a mysterious abandoned well, meets a psychic prostitute, and becomes involved in a political conspiracy.

"Sputnik Sweetheart"

The protagonist of the novel is an unnamed teacher in his thirties who becomes infatuated with a student named Sumire. Sumire, an aspiring writer, is a free-spirited and unconventional young woman. The two develop a close friendship, but Sumire's affections lie elsewhere, specifically towards an older woman named Miu.As Sumire and Miu become romantically involved, the protagonist finds himself drawn into their complex relationship. When Sumire suddenly disappears during a trip to Greece, the teacher embarks on a journey to find her, encountering mysterious and surreal events along the way.

"Kafka on the Shore"

The novel follows two parallel storylines. The first storyline revolves around a fifteen-year-old runaway named Kafka Tamura, who escapes his troubled home in search of a mysterious and prophetic entrance stone. Kafka's journey leads him to a peculiar library and a remote village where he encounters enigmatic characters and uncovers startling secrets about his own past.The second storyline centers around an elderly man named Nakata, who possesses a unique ability to communicate with cats. Nakata embarks on a quest to find a lost cat, which leads him on a surreal path intertwined with Kafka's story. Nakata's journey reveals supernatural occurrences and unexplainable events.

"1Q84"

The story of "1Q84" takes place in Tokyo during the year 1984 and follows the interconnected lives of two main characters, Aomame and Tengo. Aomame is a strong and independent woman who becomes involved in a secret organization, while Tengo is a writer who becomes entangled in a peculiar rewriting project. As their paths converge, they discover they are living in a parallel reality, a slightly altered version of 1984.

"Colorless Tsukuru Tazaki and His Years of Pilgrimage"

Tsukuru Tazaki is a quiet and unassuming individual who, in his youth, belonged to a close-knit group of friends. However, he is suddenly ostracized by the group without any explanation, leading him to a deep sense of loss and confusion. Years later, as an adult, Tsukuru embarks on a journey to uncover the truth behind his exclusion and to find resolution.The novel explores themes of identity, self-discovery, and the impact of past experiences on one's present life. Tsukuru's quest takes him on an emotional and introspective pilgrimage as he confronts his fears, confronts his past, and seeks to find meaning and connection.

"Killing Commendatore"

The story follows an unnamed portrait painter in his thirties who, after a recent divorce, moves to a remote mountain town to find solace and inspiration.While living in a house formerly owned by a famous artist, the protagonist discovers a painting hidden in the attic called "Killing Commendatore." This painting, along with a series of mysterious events and encounters, leads him into a surreal and mystical journey.
